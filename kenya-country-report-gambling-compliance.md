### RECENT INFORMATION

According to local news, in June 2015, the National Treasury Cabinet Secretary introduced the Betting, Lotteries and Gaming (Amendment) Bill. The bill seeks to introduce taxes for lotteries, gaming and prize competitions in Kenya (7.5 percent of revenues for bookmakers, 5 percent of the money collected for lotteries and 12 percent of gross revenues for casinos).

Mirroring its tourism sector, Kenya’s land-based casino market has been in decline in recent months as the sector was affected by Western travel warnings in early 2015 following a number of terrorist attacks in the country.

The Association of Gaming Operators in Kenya told GamblingCompliance that tourism was at an all-time low following travel warnings by a number of Western governments and has damaged the industry, resulting in downsizing.

The PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC) team in November 2014 estimated Kenya’s 13 strong casino industry will collectively create gross gaming revenues (GGR) of US$20.1m in 2015. The report also stated that taxes and levies are still at around US$3.2m.

After a change to the country’s constitution came into effect, the 47 provinces of Kenya, called counties, were handed autonomous powers in 2013 to regulate gambling, which was previously supervised by the Betting Control and Licensing Board (BCLB). However, the BCLB claims that national officials must be involved in licensing, as gambling is a “security issue”. It is, therefore, currently uncertain which authority is in charge of issuing licences.

- Status Regulated. Licensing regime.
- Regulations PART III of the Betting, Lotteries and Gaming Act 1966, revised edition 2012.
- Regulatory Authority The Betting Control and Licensing Board or the 47 counties (local governments).
- Operators Not available.
- Games Authorised Sports and horse race betting. Bookmaking and totalisator licences are granted.

## 2. BETTING

- Status Regulated. Licensing regime.

There are two types of casinos in Kenya: standalone casinos and casinos integrated into hotel complexes.

- Regulations PART V of the Betting, Lotteries and Gaming Act 1966, revised edition 2012.
- Regulatory Authority The Betting Control and Licensing Board or the 47 counties (local governments).

## 3. CASINOS

- Operators Around 13 operating casinos according to a PwC report ‘ Raising the stakes in Africa, Gambling outlook: 2014-2018’.
- The Association of Gaming Operators of Kenya’s website Casinos of Kenya lists 17 members.
- Status Regulated. Licensing regime.
- Regulations PART V of the Betting, Lotteries and Gaming Act 1966, revised edition 2012.
- Regulatory Authority The Betting Control and Licensing Board or the 47 counties (local governments).
- Operators Not available.

## 4. GAMING MACHINES

- Status Regulated. Single licence regime for a public lottery for charitable purpose.

Private lotteries, lotteries relating to horse racing, other races and other charitable and small lotteries may be permitted.

- Regulations PART IV of the Betting, Lotteries and Gaming Act 1966, revised edition 2012.
- Regulatory Authority The Betting Control and Licensing Board or the 47 counties (local governments).
- Operators Not available.
- Mobile Lotteries Regulated. Licensing regime.

According to The Standard, described by UK state funded BBC as Kenya’s oldest newspaper, the board collected R1.7bn in fy2013 which was filtered to the Arts, Culture and National Heritage; Charities; and Sports and Recreation.

## 5. LOTTERIES

- Status Regulated. Licensing regime.
- Mobile gambling is also authorised.
- Regulations Betting, Lotteries and Gaming Act 1966, revised edition 2012.
- Online Games Permitted Sports betting and casino games. A mobile lottery is also available.

## 6. REMOTE GAMBLING

- Scheduled Review Date Permitted
- Regulatory Authority The Betting Control and Licensing Board or the 47 counties (local governments).
- Authorised Operators The first state-run online gambling site was launched in 2011 (BetKenya.com), with Amaya Gaming providing the content platform. Mobile lotteries operators are have also been authorised.
